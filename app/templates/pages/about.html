About SoccerAlpha

SoccerAlpha sparked from one of my failed interviews where I was asked how to predict soccer games, if I use machine learning...

Ideas behind SoccerAlpha

SoccerAlpha is purely based on player's ratings from 34 attributes (more info on sofifa). In a word, SoccerAlpha is kind of an "AI" :)

Two main models are proposed - Random Forest and Convolutional Neural Network. Random Forest has roughly 60% validation accuracy and has low variance so it is currenly being used. CNN has a high variance which suggest it may need a larger training set.

How I built it

I spent some time scrapping players' data from sofifa, retrieved lineups of 380 Premier League games in season 2018~2019 from api-football, managed them in a PostgreSQL database. The reason why I use ratings of players on lineups is that it easily fits in a supervised machine learning model, and we may see dramatic feature importance between players of two teams on specific position or on specific attribute. The Random Forest model using data from a whole season gave me roughly 60% validation accuracy. There is certainly a lot to improve but for now it's a good start

Future work

I will expand the number of matches and improve performance of CNN.
